How It All Comes Together

▾

This is a Middle Eastern–inspired mezze-style lunch built around a show-stopping chicken shawarma as the centrepiece, surrounded by colourful shareable sides. Everything is dairy-free and kosher-friendly.

The shawarma does most of the work — marinate overnight, then it's a case of grilling and slicing. While that's cooking, the pickled onions are already done (make them the day before) and the quinoa salad can sit at room temperature.

The pita is last — roll and cook just before serving so they arrive warm and puffy at the table. The hummus can be made the morning of and comes together in 5 minutes with a food processor.

Key overlap: Chicken marinates overnight (passive). Quinoa roasted veg goes in the oven before the grill. Pita cooks on the hob while the chicken rests. Pickled onions are done the night before.

Chicken Shawarma

⏱ 30 min prep + 30–40 min cook🔥 Grill or Oven
▾

Ingredients

  • 8 boneless skinless chicken thighs (~700g)
  • ¼ cup shawarma spice blend (see below)
  • ⅓ cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Juice of 1 large lemon
  • Kosher salt & black pepper
  • 1 large yellow onion, thick rounds
  • 1 Roma tomato (optional, for top of spit)
  • Persian cucumbers, tomato wedges, parsley — to serve

Shawarma Spice Blend

  • 2 tsp ground cumin · 2 tsp ground coriander · 1 tsp turmeric
  • 2 tsp paprika · ½ tsp cinnamon · ½ tsp cloves · ¼ tsp cayenne (optional) · 1 tsp garlic powder

Method

  1. Marinate (day before): Whisk spice blend, olive oil, and lemon juice. Season chicken, toss to coat. Add onion rounds. Refrigerate overnight.
  2. Room temp: Remove from fridge 20–30 min before cooking.
  3. Grill: Medium-high, 10–12 min per side until charred & 74°C. Oven: 200°C, 30–40 min, turn once.
  4. Rest: 5 min at room temp. Slice thin against the grain.
💡 Tip: Chicken thighs are essential — breasts will dry out. Overnight marinade makes a huge difference.
Source: The Mediterranean Dish · Spice blend: Shawarma Spice Blend

Homemade Pita Bread

⏱ 15 min prep + 1 hr rise + 10 min cook🔥 Oven or Hob
▾

Ingredients

  • 1 cup lukewarm water · 2 tsp active dry yeast · ½ tsp sugar
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour · 1–2 tsp kosher salt · 2 tbsp olive oil

Method

  1. Sponge: Water + yeast + sugar. Wait 15 min. Add ½ cup flour, whisk. Wait 15 min until frothy.
  2. Dough: Add salt, oil, almost all flour. Knead ~2 min. Soft & slightly moist.
  3. Rise: Oiled bowl, cover. 1 hour until doubled.
  4. Divide: 7–8 balls. Rest 10 min. Roll to 8–9" wide, ¼" thick.
  5. Cook (Oven 240°C): 2 at a time on hot tray, 2 min per side. Puff! Stack under towel.
  6. Cook (Hob): Cast iron, medium-high. 30 sec, flip 1–2 min, flip 1–2 min.
💡 Tip: If pita doesn't puff, the oven/skillet isn't hot enough. Dough can be refrigerated overnight.
Source: The Mediterranean Dish

Hummus from Scratch

⏱ 5 min prep + blending🧊 Key: ice cubes
▾

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas, soaked overnight (or 2× 15oz cans)
  • 1½ tsp baking soda · ⅓ cup tahini · Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1–2 cloves garlic · ½ tsp kosher salt · 2 ice cubes
  • Olive oil, sumac, reserved chickpeas — to garnish

Method

  1. Cook: Dried: simmer 1.5–2 hrs. Canned: simmer 20 min with baking soda.
  2. Peel: Hot water + baking soda. Rub under running water. Dry. Reserve 1 tbsp.
  3. Puree: Food processor until smooth paste.
  4. Finish: Add ice cubes, tahini, salt, lemon, garlic. Blend 4–5 min until fluffy.
  5. Serve: Bowl, swirl, olive oil, sumac, reserved chickpeas.
💡 Tip: Peeling + ice cubes = restaurant-quality hummus. Don't skip either.
Source: The Mediterranean Dish

Quick Pickled Red Onions

⏱ 10 min + 30 min cooling🔴 Make the day before
▾

Ingredients

  • 2 medium red onions, very thinly sliced (~⅛ inch)
  • ½ cup water · ¼ cup distilled white vinegar · ¼ cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1½ tbsp maple syrup · 1½ tsp fine sea salt · ¼ tsp red pepper flakes (optional)

Method

  1. Pack sliced onions into a pint jar.
  2. Simmer water, vinegars, maple syrup, salt, pepper flakes.
  3. Pour hot liquid over onions. Press down to release air bubbles.
  4. Cool to room temp (20–30 min). Ready to serve.
  5. Cover & refrigerate. Best within 3 days.
💡 Tip: Don't skip the sweetener — without it, they're overly vinegary.
Source: Cookie and Kate

Quinoa Salad with Roasted Vegetables

⏱ 20 min prep + 30 min roast🌿 Dairy-free
▾

Ingredients

  • ½ cup quinoa, rinsed · 1 cup water
  • 1 medium eggplant, diced · 1 courgette, diced · 1 yellow squash, diced
  • 4 tbsp olive oil · Salt & pepper
  • Juice of 1 lemon · 1 clove garlic, minced · 1 cup halved grape tomatoes
  • ⅓ cup fresh basil · ⅓ cup fresh mint · ¼ cup sliced almonds, toasted

Method

  1. Roast veg: 220°C (425°F), 25–30 min until browned. Cool.
  2. Cook quinoa: Boil, cover, simmer 15 min, steam 5 min. Fluff.
  3. Toast nuts: Dry skillet, 4–8 min until golden.
  4. Assemble: Whisk lemon + garlic + 2 tbsp oil. Add everything. Toss gently.
  5. Serve at room temperature. Keeps 5 days in fridge.
💡 Tip: Even better after 30 min — flavours meld. Perfect make-ahead side.
Source: Cookie and Kate (adapted dairy-free)
